Analysis Methodology

This case study demonstrates a comprehensive FEA-based pressure vessel analysis following ASME Section VIII Division 2, Part 5 design-by-analysis methodology. The focus is on engineering validation rather than 3D modeling.

Finite Element Analysis Approach

Non-linear FEA using ANSYS Workbench with multi-step load cases including pressure, thermal, and seismic loads. Mesh convergence studies conducted to ensure result accuracy.

  • Element Quality: >0.7 (Skewness)
  • Mesh Refinement: Boundary layer meshing
  • Solver: Static structural with large deflection

Material Modeling

Elastic-plastic material properties with isotropic hardening. Temperature-dependent material properties considered for accurate thermal stress analysis.

  • Yield Strength: 262 MPa @ 150¬∞C
  • Tensile Strength: 483 MPa @ 150¬∞C
  • Young's Modulus: 195 GPa

Load Case Definition

Multiple load cases analyzed including design conditions, hydrotest, upset conditions, and emergency scenarios as per ASME requirements.

  • Design Pressure: 15 bar
  • Hydrotest: 19.5 bar
  • Thermal Gradient: 100¬∞C
  • Seismic: Zone 4 requirements

Boundary Conditions

Realistic support conditions with saddle supports modeled including friction and thermal expansion considerations. Nozzle loads applied per piping stress analysis.

  • Saddle Support: Fixed & sliding
  • Nozzle Loads: WRC 107/537 compliant
  • Thermal Expansion: Unrestrained

Fatigue & Cyclic Assessment

Fatigue analysis based on ASME VIII-2 Part 5 for vessels subjected to cyclic loading conditions.

Cycle Counting

Rainflow cycle counting method applied to operational pressure and temperature fluctuations over design life.

  • Design Life: 20 years
  • Pressure Cycles: 15,000
  • Thermal Cycles: 8,500
  • Startup/Shutdown: 1,200

Damage Summation

Miner's rule applied for cumulative damage calculation across all cyclic load cases.

  • Pressure Fatigue: 0.32
  • Thermal Fatigue: 0.28
  • Total Damage: 0.60
  • Design Margin: 1.67

Fatigue Life Results

Conclusion: The vessel meets the fatigue life requirements with a cumulative usage factor of 0.60, well below the allowable limit of 1.0.

Minimum Fatigue Life: 33,000 cycles (vs required 15,000 cycles)
